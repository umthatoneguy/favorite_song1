Bound for the floor by Local H

"Bound for the Floor" by Local H, released in 1996 as the lead single from their album "As Good as Dead," showcases the band's signature 1990s alternative rock style with its distinctive guitar riff and energetic sound. The song achieved significant airplay on both modern rock and mainstream rock radio stations, propelling it to No. 5 on the Billboard Modern Rock Tracks chart and No. 10 on the Mainstream Rock Tracks chart. Lyrically, the song captures themes of disillusionment and frustration, resonating deeply with the grunge and alternative rock scene of its era. Local H, comprised of Scott Lucas on guitar and vocals and Joe Daniels on drums, garnered widespread recognition and acclaim with this track, solidifying its place as one of their most popular and enduring songs.

"Bound for the Floor" in Popular Culture

"Bound for the Floor" by Local H has carved out a distinctive presence in popular culture through its appearances in various films and video games. The song featured prominently in the 2006 film "Big Nothing," starring Simon Pegg and David Schwimmer, adding to its visibility and appeal. It also found its way into the soundtrack of the 1998 movie "No Looking Back," starring Jon Bon Jovi and Edward Burns, and the 1997 Australian film "Blackrock." Additionally, gamers got a taste of its gritty sound in the Xbox 360 game "Saints Row" released the same year. These placements have not only showcased the song's versatility but also cemented its status as a memorable piece within the entertainment industry, spanning multiple mediums and capturing diverse audiences.
